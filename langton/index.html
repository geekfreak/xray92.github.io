The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly
Thursday, 18 Jan 2007 to Saturday, 24 Feb 2007

Opening reception
Thursday January 18, 7-9 pm
Admission Free

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly draws together a group of international contemporary artists whose approach to abstract painting challenges their predecessors. Presenting a gritty street-smart aesthetic or a geometrized subjectivity, their attitude towards this mode of representation is tough and transgressive, yet testifies to the continued attraction and prominence of a modernist ideality.

The artists in this exhibition embrace all the risks, doubts, and critical opprobrium inherent in taking on abstract painting. However, their industrialized drive proposes a new content while challenging a number of stereotypes associated with this ‘canonical’ language.

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly features works by Robert Bordo, Charline von Heyl, Rebecca Morris, Avery Preesman, Annabeth Rosen, Amy Sillman, and John Zurier.

The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly is guest curated by Pamela Wilson-Ryckman.
