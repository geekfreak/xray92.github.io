Network Program
 

 

NetWork is a curated showcase of net art -- art created on and for the Internet. Through this program Langton supports and presents experimental artwork that engages the Web as an artistic medium or site of cultural exchange. NetWork projects include thematic group exhibitions and commissioned projects by artists at all stages of their careers.

NetWork seeks to provide a context for critical attention to and dialogue around Internet-based art work; create a venue for the creation and presentation of new work in this evolving cultural arena; and support experimentation by contemporary artists.

From sound collage to informational maps of the web, NetWork presents artists and collaborations that employ sculpture, installation, performance, sound, video, photography and other media designs and qualities found in the infinitude of Web culture. The program invites both the artist and the viewer to continuously examine our existence with the Web.

Since its debut in 1999 NetWork projects have been presented online and in the gallery. Past NetWork participants include Lisa Jevbratt/C5 (1999), Dragan Miletic of APSOLUTNO (1999), Ed Osborn (1999), Steev Hise (2000), Masako Odaka (2000), Richard Rinehart (2000), Arijana Kaifes (2001), Lev Manovich (2001), Kevin & Jennifer McCoy (2001), Geri Wittig (2001), and Didi Dunphy (2002).
