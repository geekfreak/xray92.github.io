Gallery Program
 

 

Langton commissions and presents new work by local, national, and internation visual artists. These exhibitions include group exhibitions that address contemporary cultural issues, retrospective exhibitions, and solo and group exhibitions by both emerging and established artists, as well as interdisciplinary works. The visual arts program is presented in Langton's 2,200 square foot gallery.

Since it's inception in 1975, Langton has commission and presented works by over six thousand artists, providing the artists with the technical and promotional assistance needed to realize their works. Visual arts exhibitions have included the work of Vito Acconci (1982), Nayland Blake (1985, 93), Jessica Bronson (2001), Daniel Clowes (2001), Anna Gaskell (2001), Thomas and Lyle Ashton Harris (1998), Gary Hill, David Ireland (1989), Ned Kahn (1987, 01), Nina Katchadourian (2002), Tony Labat (1979, 82), Bernie Lubell (1989, 2000), Robert Mapplethorpe (1978), Daniel Martinez (1992), Barry McGee (1995), Adrian Piper (1993), Jim Pomeroy (1977, 84, 99), Ed "Big Daddy" Roth (1990), Kathryn Spence (1995), and Carrie Mae Weems (1993), among many others.

To submit proposals for visual arts exhibitions, see artist proposals for guidelines and more information.


Musée d'Honneur Minuscule
In addition to the visual artwork shown in the gallery, the Musée d'Honneur Minuscule presents artworks in a street level window, allowing passers-by to view the work of an emerging artist even during non-gallery hours.


SF Camerawork
From 2000-2004 Langton alternates its exhibition program with that of SF Camerawork. For more information about the programs of SF Camerawork please visit their website www.sfcamerawork.org.
