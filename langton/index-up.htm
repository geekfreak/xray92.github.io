Busted: New Works by Felipe Dulzaides & Robin Rhode
Curated by Clara Kim

June 23 - July 24
Opening Reception:

Thursday, June 24, 6-8 pm, admission free
 
 

Busted, an exhibition of new video, photography and installation by artists Felipe Dulzaides and Robin Rhode, reevaluates and reimagines the relationships between individuals and their physical surroundings.

Cuba native Dulzaides undermines the authority of everyday objects and scenarios by recontextualizing their intended meanings and uses. Whether converting a looming lifeguard chair into a toilet, or exposing the ironies of an artificial wave pool in the desert, his video and photography works embrace the humor and contradiction in unlikely superimpositions.

South Africa-born Rhode combines performance, video and photography to create bare urban narratives where people enact video game-like feats with imaginary hand-drawn props. He draws cars, basketball hoops and see-saws on the asphalt in chalk, and films them from above, positioning his actors like individual panes in a cartoon. The videos are accompanied by photographic stills illustrating each event in sequence, like pages in a flipbook frozen on the gallery walls.

Curated by Clara Kim and originating at Langton, Busted celebrates the playfully defiant spirit of these artists’ works, which tease the distinctions between interior and exterior, real and constructed, systematically challenging and reinventing the power relations embedded in public spaces.

Dulzaides and Rhode present an Artists Talk and Screening Friday, June 25 at 8 pm. Admission is $5/$3 members, students and seniors.

 

 

Gallery Hours are Tuesday through Saturday, 12 to 6 pm, and by appointment. Admission is free.
