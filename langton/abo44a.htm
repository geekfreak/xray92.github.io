Internships
 

Langton's internship program is structured to coordinate applicants' interests, backgrounds, and schedules with the needs of our organization. While we cannot offer paid internships, we offer a behind-the-scenes experience at a nonprofit arts organization, free admission to Langton events, and a chance to work with artists and arts professionals in exchange for your valuable time and talent.

Interns should be interested in working in a nonprofit gallery environment, possess strong communication skills (both in person and on the phone), and have Macintosh computer skills in word processing and email. FileMaker experience is highly desired, but training will be provided. Candidates must be able to commit eight hours a week for three months (unless noted otherwise). On-call volunteer opportunities are also available. To apply, email cover letter and resume to Gallery Manager and Office Assistant at gallery@newlangtonarts.org

INTERNSHIP POSITIONS AVAILABLE FOR SPRING 2007

CURATORIAL

Archive Intern
 The Archive Intern is interested in preserving the history of an organization and conducting archival research. Under the supervision of the Associate Curator, the intern performs research and works with archival processes. The intern organizes photos, papers and documents in the archive. The candidate must possess excellent organization, research, and FileMaker skills.

GALLERY

Gallery Intern
The Gallery Intern is interested in interfacing with the public and maintaining the presentation of a gallery. Under the supervision of the Gallery Manager, the intern maintains the database, fields phone calls, welcomes the public, answers visitor questions, and monitors the gallery. The intern gains an overview of the organization and supports all the departments as needed. The candidate must possess excellent communication and organizational skills.

Curatorial Intern
The curatorial intern is interested in learning about the different aspects of curating art and working with artists. Under the supervision of the Associate Curator, the intern researches and compiles materials of artists for upcoming shows, gathers support materials for exhibits, and helps write press releases. The candidate must possess excellent organization, research, and writing skills.

COMMUNICATIONS

Communications/Outreach Intern
The Communications/Outreach Intern is interested in press relations, outreach, and audience development. Under the supervision of the Assistant Director and Gallery Manager, the intern researches artists and programs, writes and edits copy for published materials (press releases, announcements, postcards, etc.), makes follow-up calls to press contacts, creates press kits, maintains the database, and compiles press clips. The intern also helps design and implement community outreach strategies, creates outreach materials, and tracks outreach efforts. The candidate must have strong writing skills, be comfortable on the phone, and be proficient in Word, Quark, and Filemaker.

Web Intern
The Web Intern is interested in maintaining and expanding the functions and content of Langton's web site. Under the supervision of the Gallery Manager, the intern creates and modifies graphics for use on the site and enters content into both web-based templates and the PHP database. The intern also maintains e-commerce functions of the site and assists with day-to-day production tasks to ensure information is presented in a timely and accurate manner. Some proficiency in HTML, Photoshop, and Dreamweaver necessary.

DEVELOPMENT

Development Intern
 The Development Intern is interested in learning more about fundraising and donor relations. Under the supervision of the development staff, the intern assists with all stages of the grant application process, including research, proofreading, and writing. The intern helps maintain the membership database, and assists with membership drives, the annual direct mail campaign, and special events. The candidate must possess excellent writing, communication, and organization skills. Proficiency in FileMaker Pro a plus.

PREPARATOR/OPERATIONS

Preparator and Operations Intern
The Preparator and Operations Intern is interested in the installations of exhibits and facilities maintenance. The intern works directly with the Preparator to install and de-install exhibitions, and under the supervision of the Gallery Manager with operations support, including painting and light construction in the gallery. The intern also updates and maintains the equipment inventory. A background in the arts, sculpture, construction, or theater tech is helpful, but not required. Knowledge of Filemaker or Illustrator helpful but not required. Some evening hours required.
